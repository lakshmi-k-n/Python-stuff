As an artist, Jay-Z holds the record for most number one albums by a solo artist on the US Billboard 200 with 14.[16][17] He has also had four number ones on the Billboard Hot 100, one ("Empire State of Mind") as lead artist. In 2009, he was ranked the tenth-most successful artist of the 2000s by Billboard as well as the fifth top solo male artist and fourth top rapper behind Eminem, Nelly, and 50 Cent.[18] He was also ranked the 88th-greatest artist of all time by Rolling Stone.[19]
